TARS: Training Adaptive Reasoners for Safety

Reasoning as an Adaptive Defense for Safety

Taeyoun Kim1, Fahim Tajwar2, Aditi Raghunathan2, Aviral Kumar2,
1Carnegie Mellon University
Paper arXiv Model

Main Figure

Abstract

Large language models have shown remarkable capabilities in reasoning and problem-solving, but ensuring their safety remains a critical challenge. We introduce TARS (Training Adaptive Reasoners for Safety), a novel approach that trains models to dynamically adapt their reasoning strategies based on safety considerations. Our method leverages reinforcement learning from human feedback to develop adaptive reasoning mechanisms that can identify potentially harmful outputs and redirect toward safer alternatives. Through extensive experiments on safety benchmarks, we demonstrate that TARS significantly improves safety performance while maintaining competitive capabilities on standard reasoning tasks. The adaptive nature of our approach allows models to handle diverse safety scenarios without sacrificing general reasoning abilities.
